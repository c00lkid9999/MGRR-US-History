Submission 9: Cuban Missile Crisis News Anchor Script

10/28/1962

ANCHOR: Breaking news: Today, the Cuban Missile Crisis, which we have all been closely following, ended after Soviet leader Khrushchev conceded from the conflict and removed the Soviet missiles from Cuba.

This terrifying event started a mere 14 days ago, when a U.S. U-2 spy plane, piloted by Major Richard Heyser, took photos of newly built installations in the Cuban countryside. The very next day, CIA analysts spotted launchers, missiles, and transport trucks in those photos. This indicated that the Soviets were building sites to launch missiles at targets all across our country.

President Kennedy quickly met with his advisers to discuss how to respond to this threat. They all offered varying opinions, and Kennedy decided against attacking Cuba and opted to call a quarantine in the attempt to negotiate a withdrawal.

If you recall his television speech on our network on October 22nd, JFK revealed the evidence of this threat and announced that the United States would enact the previously mentioned quarantine, preventing ships with weapons from reaching Cuba and demanding the Soviets to withdraw their missiles. Throughout the next few days, we waited for news of the negotiations as the world leaders communicated.

Just yesterday, you will remember that a different U-2 pilot than the one that took the photos was shot down and killed over Cuba. This put the conflict into a whole new perspective, as the Soviets had potentially fired the first shot in a nuclear war. This led both leaders to realize that the situation was out of control, and the United States revealed that they would also remove their missiles from Turkey.

Today, peace was reached and the missiles will quickly be removed from Cuba. This event will be remembered in history as a near miss to nuclear war, and our country and our president’s solid stance in the face of outright Soviet aggression.

Submission 10: Cold War Map and Analysis

  1. Bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, Japan, 1945

    1. This was the first use of nuclear weapons by the United States to end WWII.

    2. This increased tensions with the Soviet Union, setting the stage for the Cold War.

  2. Soviet Union tests first nuclear weapon, Semipalatinsk Test Site, Kazakhstan, 1949

    1. This test came years ahead of American predictions

    2. It triggered a nuclear arms race between the two superpowers

  3. US tests first hydrogen bomb, Eniwetok Atoll, Marshall Islands, 1952

    1. Tested in the Marshall Islands, 3,000 miles west of Hawaii

    2. Fireball was 3 miles wide, mushroom cloud was 100 miles wide

    3. Yield of explosion was 10 megatons

  4. Korean War, 1950-1953

    1. After US and USSR left Korea, friction between the two sides escalated into war

    2. The South was immediately overrun, but the United States helped them

    3. Eventually, peace was reached in 1953 with a no-man’s land being set as the new border.

  5. Vietnam War, 1959-1975

    1. Important sphere of influence by both US and Soviet leaders

    2. USSR and China backed new communist country, US wanted to combat it

    3. North Vietnam ultimately won

  6. Largest nuclear weapon ever built, Big Ivan / Tsar Bomba, tested, Novaya Zemlya, Russia, October 30, 1961

    1. 100 megaton hydrogen bomb

    2. Detonated at 13,000 feet above Novaya Zemlya island, fireball reached the ground

    3. Blast pressure was 300 psi, mushroom cloud reached altitude of 210,000 feet

  7. Cuban Missile Crisis, 1962

    1. Occurred when a American spy plane took photographs of nuclear missile launch sites in Cuba

    2. Brought the United States and the Soviet Union the closest ever to Nuclear War

Hot Location A: Berlin

  1. Berlin Blockade and Airlift, 1948-1949

    1. The USSR attempted to gain control of all of Berlin by cutting of surface access to the west side

    2. The United States responded by airlifting supplies to them daily

  2. Berlin Wall constructed, begins on August 13, 1961

    1. Created to prevent East Berliners from fleeing to West Berlin

    2. Initially barbed wire and concrete fence, replaced with a 13-foot tall wall

  3. Berlin Wall torn down, 1989

    1. Came just a year after Ronald Reagan gave his famous “Tear down this wall” speech

    2. Millions of Germans celebrated this occasion

Hot Location B: United States

  1. Titan II ICBMS deployed

    1. Titan II was the largest land-based missile ever developed by the US.

    2. It was an important component of US Strategy.

    3. 54 missiles were deployed.

  2. Last Titan II comes off alert, Little Rock, Arkansas, May 5, 1987

    1. Stayed in its underground silo for almost 24 years

Hot Location C: Soviet Union

  1. Soviet Union collapses, 1991

    1. Leaders of every Eastern European nation except Bulgaria were overthrown by the end of 1989

    2. After several attempts to overthrow Gorbachev, the Soviet Union collapsed.

Linkage i: Relations between USA and USSR

  1. Space Race begins, 1957

    1. Both US and USSR wanted technological superiority

    2. The Soviets made the first satellite, Sputnik. The US thought that meant that the USSR had the missile technology to attack them with nuclear weapons.

  2. Hotline established between USA and USSR, post-Cuban Missile Crisis

    1. Referred to as the “Red Phone”

    2. Transmitted written messages to reduce the chance of misunderstandings between the two countries

    3. US used the Hot Line in 1967 during the Arab-Israeli War to explain fleet movements in the Mediterranean

  3. Able Archer and the War Scare, 1983

    1. Closest the United States and USSR got to war besides Cuban Missile Crisis

    2. Soviet Union interpreted routine war preparation exercise of United States (“Able Archer”) as a prelude to a first strike
