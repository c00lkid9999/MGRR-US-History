Submission 6: Vietnam War Timeline

June 1, 1954: The Saigon Military Mission is launched in South Vietnam under the command of the U.S. Air Force Col. Edward Lansdale, marking the beginning of the Vietnam War.

July 21, 1954: Vietnam is divided in two at the 17th parallel.

November 2, 1963: Ngo Dihn Diem is assassinated by his own generals, a part of a coup d’état carried out with the support of US officials. This was due to his poor and excessively violent treatment of South Vietnam’s majority Buddhist population.

August 5, 1964: Gulf of Tonkin incident: Commanders report a North Vietnamese torpedo attack on US destroyers, causing President Lyndon B. Johnson to submit the Gulf of Tonkin Resolution to Congress, allowing him to “take all necessary measures to repel any armed attack against the forces of the United States.”

March 1, 1966: A Program for the Pacification and Long-Term Development of Vietnam (PROVN) is published, and gives helpful feedback on the war in Vietnam. However, it is largely ignored by US commanders.

March 16, 1968: Mai Lai massacre: US army troops kill up to 500 unarmed villagers in the hamlet of Mai Lai. The soldiers attempt to cover it up almost immediately, and only one American is found guilty of any crime relating to it.

November 15, 1969: Millions of people across the US protest the continued US involvement in Vietnam - one of the largest public protest in US history to date.

January 27, 1973: The Agreement on Ending the War and Restoring Peace in Viet-Name is concluded, leaving the dividing line in vietnam until the country can be reunited in a peaceful way.

March 29, 1973: The last US military unit leaves Vietnam.

April 29, 1975: Saigon is evacuated as an emergency maneuver and American and South Vietnamese are flown to safety. The next morning, North Vietnamese troops enter downtown Saigon and South Vietnam surrenders.
